Privacy Policy

At ResQAI, protecting user data during critical emergency situations is a core design principle. This policy outlines how we collect, process, store, and safeguard information while delivering real-time crisis intelligence.

1. Data Collection & Purpose

ResQAI collects only the minimum data required to deliver life-saving assistance. This may include location data, system interaction logs, and emergency inputs provided by the user. The sole purpose of data collection is to enable rapid response guidance, incident detection, and situational awareness during crisis scenarios.

2. Data Anonymization

During SOS activation and AI-assisted emergency guidance, all processed data is anonymized wherever possible. Identifiable user attributes are either removed or abstracted to ensure privacy while maintaining operational efficiency. Our AI systems prioritize speed and accuracy over personal identification.

3. AI Processing & Third-Party Services

ResQAI utilizes multiple AI providers including OpenRouter, Gemini, and Groq to ensure high availability and redundancy. Data sent to these services is strictly limited to contextual emergency inputs. No persistent personal identifiers are shared with external AI providers.

4. Local & Secure Storage

System state, logs, and emergency-related data are stored securely using SQLite or MySQL depending on deployment scale. Data is isolated per organization (e.g., hotel, institution) ensuring strict tenant-level separation and preventing cross-access between systems.

5. Incident Analytics & Usage

Aggregated and anonymized data may be used for analytics, including response time optimization, incident pattern recognition, and system performance improvements. These insights are strictly used to enhance safety protocols and are accessible only to authorized personnel.

6. Access Control & Authorization

Access to sensitive data and system dashboards is restricted through role-based permissions. Administrative actions such as system creation or analytics access require authenticated sessions, while emergency usage remains accessible without login to ensure zero friction during crises.

7. Data Retention Policy

Emergency data is retained only for as long as necessary to support operational needs and improve system intelligence. Temporary logs may be automatically cleared or archived based on system configuration and organizational policies.

8. Security Measures

We implement industry-standard security practices including encrypted data transmission (HTTPS), secure API handling, and database isolation. Continuous monitoring and fallback systems ensure resilience even during partial infrastructure failures.

9. User Responsibility

Users are responsible for providing accurate information during emergencies. Misuse of the system, including false alerts or unauthorized access attempts, may result in restricted access or administrative action by system operators.

10. Policy Updates

This privacy policy may be updated periodically to reflect improvements in system architecture, security practices, or compliance requirements. Continued use of ResQAI indicates acceptance of the latest version of this policy.
